SO HOW DO YOU FIND JOBS?

There are many ways, here are a few...

1. JOB SEARCH ENGINES

There are many job search engines that enlist all jobs available in a specific field and area. They are the easiest way to find jobs and are very convenient. Here are some that you might want to try:

2. ALUMNI ASSOCIATIONS

Your university probably has an Alumni Association that has ample resources to help Alumni find jobs. Try getting in contact with them through their website. You will be surprised by how much you will learn from them. Here's a link to UCLA's Alumni association:

3. COMPANY WEBSITES

If you know what field you want to work in, you can look for a job in a company's website. For example, if you want to work in the computer science field, you could look for a job on Microsoft's website. You will usually find a link such as "careers" which will lead you to an employment page. Here's an example on Microsoft's website:


The link should lead you to a page like this.


4. STATE EMPLOYMENT RESOURCES

Most states usually have resources such as job banks and phone and web directories. It is a good idea to look through these even if you have found a viable job using the other resources. Here's California's job resource website:
